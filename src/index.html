Me

Hi! I'm Dana, a human product designer. This is a space where I share |

The spark

In 2020, I became a Requirements Engineer for a car dealership app.

While "translating" product requirements into detailed specs for the engineering team something caught my attention, the product design team.

They were shaping the product, defining user experiences, and driving key decisions. I watched them transform abstract ideas into intuitive designs, and for the first time, I saw the bigger picture. I realised I didn’t want to just document a product, I wanted to help create it.

I started looking into product design, user experience, interaction patterns, and how great products are built. What began as frustration with my role turned into the spark that set me on this path.

The leap

Once I knew what I wanted to do, the next challenge was figuring out how to get there.

I enrolled in a bootcamp, poured everything into learning, and convinced myself that by the end of it, I’d be a great (junior) hire for any company. I was wrong. Not about being a good candidate, but about how difficult it would be to land a job.

A saturated job marked, companies prioritised experienced designers, and the rare junior roles were flooded with applications just like me, all fighting for the same opportunities. Standing out felt nearly impossible.

I kept applying, refining my skills, and learning on my own. I also completed three internship programs, each shaping my perspective on design and strengthening my confidence. It was a long road, but eventually, I landed the job I had been working so hard for.

Designing for enterprise

In the summer of 2022, I joined a global leader in enterprise automation.
My primary focus is on enterprise web design.

Being part of a company that has thousands of employees and a strong presence worldwide has shaped me significantly:

  • Collaborating across time zones with diverse teams.
  • Partnering with engineers and PMs to bring designs to life.
  • Presenting work regularly, icorporating feedback, and iterating.
  • Contributing to an evolving design system while pushing for innovation.
  • Balancing high expectations of quality with technical constraints.
  • Applying big-picture thinking to anticipate challenges.
  • Managing complexity while maintaining clarity.
  • Adapting to shifting priorities with focus.
  • Embracing a culture of continuous learning and improvement.

Mastering complexity

Designing for a complex B2B platform comes with it's challenges.

Platform complexity through incorporating different products, various workflows, interdependencies, and the need to support diverse user personas in their end-to-end journeys.

It has been challenging to balance the needs of highly experienced, technical users who expect access to detailed information about their systems with those of less technical users who need intuitive navigation and guidance.

Design complexity also arises. Multiple designers working on a wide range of features, each product within the platform with a different set of priorities that must ultimately align for a cohesive experience.

To address these challenges, I focus on clear communication. I'm not afraid to ask questions, share my work and ask for alignment syncs to ensure consistency and collaboration across teams.

Sample work items

Monitoring dashboards

How to consolidate and present key data in a single, accessible interface to help users interpret trends, detect issues and make informed decisions quickly.

Keywords: data visualization, real-time metrics, UX clarity, performance monitoring, alerting, user-centric design

Configuration wizards

How to simplify complex setup flows by unifying disconnected steps into a guided, cohesive process, reducing cognitive load and user error.

Keywords: progressive disclosure, step-by-step flows, onboarding, setup automation, user guidance, task simplification

Contextual experiences

How to enable users to dive into specific content or make detailed edits without losing their place, maintaining context and minimizing disruptions.

Keywords: live previews, side panels, embedded workflows, inline editing, form-in-form, contextual UI, multitasking support

Data grids & filtering enhancements

How to improve data discoverability and manipulation by enhancing grid usability and introducing powerful filtering, sorting, and search options, tailored for both novice and advanced users.

Keywords: data tables, smart filtering, multi-criteria sorting, inline controls, search UX, column customization, user productivity

Mobile responsiveness

How to adapt complex desktop web patterns, such as multi-layer tab navigation and dense interfaces for mobile screens, ensuring usability, accessibility, and performance across devices.

Keywords: responsive design, mobile-first, adaptive UI, collapsible navigation, gesture support, viewport optimization, usability on small screens
